Morocco Tips

🕌

Morocco Introduction

Payments

Currency is Moroccan Dirham (MAD). ATMs are widely available. Credit cards accepted at most hotels and restaurants.

Customs & Etiquette

Morocco is a Muslim country. Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques. Remove shoes when entering homes. Use your right hand for eating and greeting.

👁️

Things to See

  • Medina and souks
  • Majorelle Garden
  • Bahia Palace
  • Koutoubia Mosque
  • Atlas Mountains day trip
  • Saadian Tombs
🍽️

Food & Drink

Things to Eat

  • Tagine (chicken, lamb, or vegetarian)
  • Couscous (traditionally served on Fridays)
  • Pastilla (sweet and savory pastry)
  • Mint tea (served throughout the day)
  • Fresh orange juice
  • Moroccan pastries and sweets

Where to Drink

  • Hotel bars and terraces
  • Sky Bar (rooftop views)
  • Traditional coffee houses
  • Gueliz district establishments
  • Hivernage area lounges

Note: Alcohol is available but not as prevalent due to Islamic culture.

Children & Nannies

Children are welcome at all events. Professional nanny services can be arranged upon request through RSVP.

Dietary Requirements

We will be serving a set menu featuring authentic Moroccan cuisine. While the menu is pre-planned, we'll do our best to accommodate any dietary restrictions or allergies you specify in your RSVP.

Language

Most hotels and venues speak English, and French is widely understood throughout Morocco. Learning a few basic Arabic phrases like "Shukran" (thank you) and "Marhaba" (hello) goes a long way with locals!
